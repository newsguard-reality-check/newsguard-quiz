🔍 How to spot a fake screenshot
Social media screenshots are easy to fabricate. Here are the key things to check before sharing or believing one.
🔗
Go to the source. Search for the post on the actual platform. For Trump's Truth Social posts, you can use trumpstruth.org — an archive of every post he has made.
🖋️
Check the writing style. Does the language, tone, and phrasing match what the account typically posts? Unusual formality, spelling, or vocabulary are red flags.
🔡
Look for font and formatting inconsistencies. Fake screenshots often have subtle differences in font weight, spacing, or button layout compared to genuine posts.
📅
Cross-check the date and context. Does the claimed date match other events? Was the person even active on the platform that day?
📰
See if credible outlets reported it. If a post is newsworthy, journalists will have covered it. No coverage is a strong signal the post may be fake.
🔍
Do a reverse image search. Use Google Images or TinEye to check if the screenshot has appeared elsewhere, and in what context.
