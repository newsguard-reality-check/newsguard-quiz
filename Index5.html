About America's Last Line of Defense

While individual posts published by America's Last Line of Defense are not labeled as satire, the Facebook page states in its "Intro" section that it is part of a "network of trollery and propaganda for cash. Nothing on this page is real." However, many of the claims that originate on this page spread on other accounts that do not label the claims as satire.

Tips for identifying satire
🔍
Look for the watermark
Many ALLOD satire claims spread through graphics that have a small watermark in the bottom left corner labeling them as satire. If you see that watermark on an image, it originated from ALLOD.
🗞️
Search for the claim in reliable sources
Do a quick Google search and see if the story has been reported by established news outlets. If you're only seeing Facebook and X links, that's a bad sign — real news gets covered by real newsrooms.
🎭
Check the source's "About" section
Satire pages like ALLOD often disclose their nature in an "About" or "Intro" section most readers never see. A quick check of the page's bio can reveal that "nothing on this page is real."
🤔
Be skeptical of stories that confirm your suspicions
Satire often leans into absurdity — extreme dollar amounts, implausible scenarios, or claims that perfectly confirm partisan assumptions. If a headline makes you feel instantly vindicated, it warrants extra scrutiny.
📅
Check the date
Some satire stories resurface years after their original publication, stripped of context. A story about a politician doing something outrageous may have originated as a joke long ago and been reshared as if current.
